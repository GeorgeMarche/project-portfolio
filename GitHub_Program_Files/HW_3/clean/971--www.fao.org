It provides market information on four grains particularly important in international food markets: wheat, maize, rice and soybeans.\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nFood and Agriculture Microdata Catalogue (FAM)\nThe Food and Agriculture Microdata (FAM) Catalogue provides access to micro datasets collected through farm and household surveys which contain information related to agriculture, food security and nutrition.\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n  \n\nFishstat\nFishery and Aquaculture\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n  \n\nAquastat\nWater resources and management\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n  \n\nFPMA\nFood to food and agriculture, from hunger and malnutrition to rural poverty, from food systems productivity to the sustainable use of natural resources or to climate change.\n\nFAO is dedicated to collecting, analysing, interpreting and disseminating food and agriculture statistics that are relevant for decision-making. \n\nIt is for this reason that developing and implementing methodologies and standards to assist countries in generating sound data and information is at the core of FAO\xe2\x80\x99s statistical work. \n\nFAO also supports member countries in the collection, dissemination and uptake of data to formulate policies and plans or to better orient investments.Major areas of FAO\xe2\x80\x99s statistical workA comprehensive list of FAO Statistical activities is included in the
