State Religion</h1><div class="entry-meta"> <span class="entry-date"><a href="http://thegarrisoncenter.org/archives/15412" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2020-09-29T09:01:37-04:00">September 29, 2020</time></a></span> <span class="byline"><span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://thegarrisoncenter.org/archives/author/thomas-l-knapp" rel="author">Thomas L. Knapp</a></span></span> <span class="comments-link"><a href="http://thegarrisoncenter.org/archives/15412#respond">Leave a comment</a></span></div><!-- .entry-meta --></header><!-- .entry-header --><div class="entry-content"><figure id="attachment_15022" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-15022" style="width: 640px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img class="size-full wp-image-15022" src="http://thegarrisoncenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/two-people-showing-fear-on-coronavirus-3952207.jpg" alt="Photo by cottonbro from Pexels" width="640" height="426" srcset="http://thegarrisoncenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/two-people-showing-fear-on-coronavirus-3952207.jpg 640w, http://thegarrisoncenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/two-people-showing-fear-on-coronavirus-3952207-300x200.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /><figcaption id="caption-attachment-15022" class="wp-caption-text">Photo by cottonbro from Pexels</figcaption></figure><p>The TL;DR on COVID-19: Panic, not science, continues to drive the public policy discussion.</p><p>Here, such as it is, is the current science:</p><p>If 100,000 Americans  age 19 or younger contract COVID-19, three of them will die.</p><p>If 10,000 Americans between 20 and 49 years old contract COVID-19, two of them will die.</p><p>If a thousand Americans between 50 and 69 years old contract COVID-19, five of them will die.</p><p>But if you&#8217;re 70 years or older and contract COVID-19, your chances of dying skyrocket to about 1 in 20.</p><p>Those are the <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/hcp/planning-scenarios.html#box1" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">&#8220;best estimates&#8221;</a> of COVID-19 Infection Fatality Ratios published by the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.</p><p>If the CDC&#8217;s estimates are correct &#8212; and if I had a nickel for every time I&#8217;ve heard &#8220;listen to the experts!&#8221; I&#8217;d need a bigger house to store my nickels in &#8212; a COVID-19 infection is less likely than a seasonal flu infection to kill anyone under 70.</p><p>There&#8217;s a caveat, of course: CDC estimates COVID-19&#8217;s &#8220;R0&#8221; (the average number of people an infected person infects in turn) at 2.5, while seasonal flu&#8217;s R0 runs about half that. COVID-19 spreads more quickly and easily than flu. More cases equals more deaths.</p><p>And there&#8217;s an excuse: These are the numbers we have NOW, not the numbers we had when the pandemic hit. What we had THEN was uncertainty.</p><p>But uncertainty isn&#8217;t a very good excuse for shutting down significant portions of the US economy, driving unemployment up from one in 25 American workers to one in seven (it&#8217;s still nearly one in ten), and placing much of the population under house arrest.</p><p>When we didn&#8217;t know what was going on, panic wasn&#8217;t the correct answer.</p><p>Now that we have a better idea of what&#8217;s happening, holding onto the visible vestiges of panic isn&#8217;t the correct answer either. It&#8217;s just a new, state-imposed religion.</p><p>As late as a few months ago, the US regime condemned governments which require women to cover their faces in public.</p><p>Now, American politicians command men and women alike to do so, and most Americans meekly submit &#8212; not because masks prevent the spread of COVID-19 (evidence for that claim is sketchy at best), but because politicians and the &#8220;scientific&#8221; bureaucratic priesthood live to issue orders and obeying those orders lets us feel like we&#8217;re &#8220;doing something&#8221; about COVID-19.</p><p>Thus the masks, and the special magic &#8220;social distancing&#8221; formulae, and the ubiquitous &#8220;Stay Home, Stay Safe&#8221; signage. The Cult of the Omnipotent State is enjoying a periodic revival unlike any since 9/11.</p><p>If the CDC&#8217;s numbers are correct, they offer two lessons:</p><p>First, protecting the elderly from COVID-19 as best we can makes sense.</p><p>Second, the rest of us need to abandon the state-imposed superstitions and get back to living in the real world.</p><p><em>Thomas L. He lives and works in north central Florida.</em></p><p><strong>PUBLICATION/CITATION HISTORY</strong></p><div class="sharedaddy sd-sharing-enabled"><div class="robots-nocontent sd-block sd-social
